This project analyzes how major economic and geopolitical events impact different stock sectors.

The motivation for this project comes from recent geopolitical tensions such as the U.S.–Iran conflict, which has led to a sharp rise in global energy prices. Oil prices surged rapidly due to supply disruptions and risks to key transit routes like the Strait of Hormuz. This created strong market movements, particularly in the energy sector, and triggered fear of missing out (FOMO) among investors.

This project aims to answer:

How do major economic/ geopolitical events impact different stock sectors? Do those impacts last or just short-term hype?

Key findings (based on 17 years of data across 9 US sector ETFs and 5 major events):
- Event-driven sector spikes are mostly short-term, reverting toward their structural baseline within 3 to 6 months.
- Energy is the clearest FOMO trap, with the biggest event-period spikes, but gains fade quickly with high volatility.
- Technology and Healthcare are the consistent long-term outperformers, with Technology leading on growth and Healthcare offering the best risk-adjusted returns.
- Real Estate and Financials are the most structurally vulnerable, with slow recovery after credit-driven crises.
- Utilities and Consumer act as defensive anchors — mild drawdowns and predictable recovery.
